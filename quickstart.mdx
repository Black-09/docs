---
title: "HCEGuard Quick Start: Register, Sign In, and Play"
description: "Step-by-step guide to create an account, sign in with your OTP, enable Halo CE integration, and start playing on a protected server in minutes."
---

This guide walks you through everything from creating your account to launching Halo CE on a protected server. The whole process takes about five minutes on a fresh install. If you have not installed HCEGuard yet, start with the [installation guide](/installation).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register your account">
    Open the HCEGuard registration page from the link your server administrator provides, or from the **Register** option inside the app. Fill in the required details to create your player account.

    Your nickname must be alphanumeric and no longer than 11 characters — for example, `Player01` or `BlackWolf`. You will use this exact nickname every time you sign in.

    <Note>
      Registration happens on the HCEGuard web portal, not inside the desktop app. Check with your server's community for the registration link.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install and open HCEGuard">
    If you have not installed HCEGuard yet, follow the [installation guide](/installation) to download the app and install the .NET 8 Desktop Runtime.

    Once installed, double-click **HCEGuard.exe**. The login screen appears with two fields: **Nick** and **OTP Code**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with your nickname and OTP">
    Enter your registered nickname in the **Nick** field. Nicknames are alphanumeric and case-sensitive, with a maximum length of 11 characters.

    Open your authenticator app or check the channel where your OTP is delivered, then enter the current **6-digit code** in the **OTP Code** field. OTP codes are numeric only.

    Optionally, check **Remember me** to have your nickname pre-filled the next time you open HCEGuard.

    Click **Enter** (or press `Enter` on your keyboard) to sign in.

    <Warning>
      After three consecutive failed login attempts, a CAPTCHA appears. Solve it to continue. The CAPTCHA resets automatically after a successful login.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your session is active">
    After a successful login, the form fields disappear and the screen shows a welcome message with your nickname — for example, **Welcome, Player01** — and a confirmation that the session started successfully.

    The **Sign out** button replaces the **Enter** button. Your session is now active and HCEGuard is communicating with the server in the background.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Halo CE integration (optional)">
    If the server you want to join requires Halo CE integration, you need to enable it and point the app to your Halo CE installation folder.

    Open **Settings** from the title bar. Toggle on **Enable Halo CE integration**. Then click **Browse** next to the **Halo CE path** field and select the folder where Halo CE is installed — the folder that contains `haloce.exe`.

    Once you save the path, HCEGuard generates the local validation files it needs and begins monitoring your session.

    <Note>
      If you see a warning that Halo CE is already running, close the game completely before enabling integration. HCEGuard must be signed in and integration must be active before you launch Halo CE.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch Halo CE">
    With HCEGuard running and your session active, open Halo CE normally. HCEGuard runs in the background and keeps your authentication valid while you play.

    If you see a notification that Halo CE was already open when you logged in, close the game, wait a moment, and reopen it so the server validation update takes effect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens when you sign out or close the app

Clicking **Sign out** or closing HCEGuard ends your local session and notifies the server. If you close the app while a session is active, a confirmation dialog appears first. Closing the app does not ban or suspend your account — you can sign in again at any time.

If more than one instance of Halo CE is detected while integration is active, HCEGuard will automatically disable the integration and end your session as a security measure. Close the extra Halo CE instances and sign in again to resume.
